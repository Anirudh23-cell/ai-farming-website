📚 Food Safety Education

Understand certifications, standards, and best practices for safe food in Hyderabad

🏛️ All food businesses in Hyderabad must comply with the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) regulations under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006.
📜
FSSAI License
Mandatory for all food businesses. Look for the 14-digit FSSAI number displayed at the premises. Verify at fssai.gov.in. License must be renewed annually.
Read more ↓
🏥
Health & Hygiene Certificate
Issued by Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation (GHMC). Required for all food handlers. Includes medical fitness certificate for staff.
Read more ↓
🌡️
Temperature Standards
Cold foods below 5°C. Hot foods above 60°C. The "danger zone" (5–60°C) allows rapid bacterial growth. Critical for Hyderabad's hot climate.
Read more ↓
💧
Water Quality Standards
Restaurants must use treated potable water for cooking and cleaning. Regular testing for coliform bacteria is mandatory under GHMC rules.
Read more ↓
🧤
Personal Hygiene
Food handlers must wash hands frequently, wear gloves, hair nets, and clean uniforms. No jewellery while handling food. No eating or smoking in food prep areas.
Read more ↓
🐛
Pest Control
Premises must have documented pest control treatments. Screens on windows, sealed drainage, and proper waste management are required by FSSAI.
Read more ↓
📜 FSSAI License — Detailed Guide +
FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) licenses are mandatory under the FSS Act 2006. There are three types: Registration (turnover ≤₹12 lakhs/year), State License (₹12 lakhs–₹20 crores), and Central License (₹20 crores+). The 14-digit license number must be displayed at every food premise. Consumers can verify any FSSAI license at foscos.fssai.gov.in. Always check for expiry date. Non-compliant businesses face penalties up to ₹5 lakhs or imprisonment.
🏥 Health Certificate — What to Know +
GHMC (Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation) issues annual health certificates to food establishments. Inspectors check: kitchen cleanliness, storage conditions, drainage, ventilation, pest control records, and food handler medical certificates. Restaurants are graded A (excellent), B (good), C (satisfactory), or D (unsatisfactory). Grade A and B certificates are displayed as green stickers. Always look for the grade display near the entrance.
🌡️ Temperature Control in Hyderabad's Climate +
Hyderabad's hot climate (average 26–40°C) means extra vigilance is required. Cold chain must never be broken. Dairy, meat, and cooked rice are high-risk foods. Never leave cooked food at room temperature for more than 2 hours (1 hour if ambient temp exceeds 32°C). Biriyani must be served immediately or kept above 60°C. Street food vendors must use insulated containers for hot foods.
💧 Water Safety for Food Use +
Only municipal-treated water or RO-treated water should be used in food preparation. Ice used in beverages must be made from potable water. Water storage tanks must be cleaned every 6 months and covered to prevent contamination. Restaurants near construction zones must test water more frequently. Report suspected water contamination to GHMC Helpline: 040-21111111.
🧤 Personal Hygiene Standards +
All food handlers must undergo annual medical examination and carry a valid health card. Handwashing for 20 seconds with soap before handling food, after restroom use, after touching face, money, or phone. Cuts and wounds must be covered with brightly colored (visible) waterproof bandages. No smoking within food premises. Long hair must be tied and covered. Nail polish and false nails are prohibited in food service roles.
🐛 Pest Control Requirements +
Licensed pest control treatment is required at least quarterly for large food establishments and every 6 months for smaller ones. Records of pest control treatments must be available for inspection. Food must be sealed and removed from areas being treated. Rodent bait stations must not be placed in food storage areas. Report cockroach or rodent sightings in food establishments using our "Report Safety Concern" form.
